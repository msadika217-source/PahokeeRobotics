1.Acronym to check if it is a stroke?

F.A.S.T
S.T.R.O.K.E

22. In F.A.S.T., what does the 'F' stand for?

Face Drooping
Fast Heartbeat

3. What does the 'A' stand for?

Arm Weakness
Airway Blockage

4. What does the 'S' stand for?

Speech Difficulty
Sudden Shaking

5. What does the 'T' stand for?

Time to call 911
Temperature check

6. Why is a stroke called a "Brain Attack"?

Blood flow to the brain is cut off
The brain is over-oxygenated

7. If symptoms go away quickly, do you still need a doctor?

Yes, it could be a warning stroke (TIA)
No, it was just a glitch

8. Can a stroke cause sudden vision loss?

Yes
No

9. Should you give a stroke victim food or water?

No, they may have trouble swallowing
Yes, to keep them hydrated

10. Which law protects you when you try to help?

Good Samaritan Law
The Medical Privacy Act

2. What should you ask a person to do to check for "Arm weakness"?

Raise both arms

3. Which is a common sign of speech difficulty during a stroke?

Slurred or strange-sounding speech

4. Why is "Time" so critical in the F.A.S.T. method?

Brain cells die quickly without blood flow
To ensure the person doesn't fall asleep

5. If you notice even one sign of a stroke, what should you do?

Call 911 immediately

6. How does the Good Samaritan Law help bystanders?

8. How should you position a conscious stroke victim while waiting for help?

Lying on their side with head slightly raised

9. Why should you NOT give a stroke victim food or water?

10. What is the "Golden Hour" in stroke treatment?
